Student Design Challenge

Specifications

    Motors: 300 rpm, 12V DC motors, SG90 Micro servo motor for capture door
    Controllers: 2.4 GHz rf wireless Bluetooth sp2 controller
    Microcontroller: Arduino Mega
    Motor drivers: L298N
    Wheels: 58mm omni wheels
    Battery: 2400mAh LiPo
    No. of bots: 4, 1 goalkeeper, 1 striker, 2 players
    Chassis material: 0.5mm Aluminum sheet

Description

With the specification to make as many soccerbots as possible, given that they fit a 50cm x 50cm starting square, Altair decided to make 4 optimally sized bots, each driven on 3 omni wheels, to give swift multi directional motion (like a soccer player) instead of a manually controlled differential drive. The capture mechanism on 3 of the robots was a door that shut down once the ball is in the cavity, controlled using a servo motor. The goalkeeper was equipped with a special extended top to defend higher goals. The striker had a striking mechanism, an amalgamation of a rack and pinion and a spring, giving it the ability to shoot at a very high speed. Every component of the mechanism was designed, prototyped and made by us in our own lab using our self-developed 3D printer. Our team was the only one having a shooting mechanism. The Arduino mega along with 2 motor drivers and a 2400mAh LiPo battery, were used to connect and power the 3 DC motors, 1 servo motor and the Bluetooth connector for the controller. All the electronics were concealed within the two plates of the robot to provide protection and safety from clashes, making them sturdier.
